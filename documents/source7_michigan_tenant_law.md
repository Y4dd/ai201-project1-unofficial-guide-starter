# Michigan Tenant Rights — Security Deposit Law

Source: Michigan Security Deposit Act (Act 348), MCL 554.609–554.613
Reference: hemlane.com/resources/michigan-security-deposit-laws

---

## The Core Rule Every Student Must Know

Michigan law gives your landlord exactly **30 days** after you move out to either return your full deposit OR send you an itemized list of damages. If they miss this deadline, they lose all right to keep any of it — and owe you double.

---

## Maximum Deposit Limit

Landlords may charge a security deposit of **no more than 1.5 times the monthly rent**.

- Example: $900/month rent → max deposit is $1,350
- Any deposit above this limit is illegal

---

## The 30-Day Return Deadline

After a tenant vacates, the landlord has **30 days** to:
1. Return the full deposit, OR
2. Return the remaining amount after deductions AND provide a written itemized damage list

**Critical detail:** The 30-day clock does not start until the landlord receives the tenant's **written forwarding address**. Always send your forwarding address in writing (certified mail is best).

---

## What the Itemized Damage List Must Include

If the landlord withholds any amount, the written notice must contain:
- The **specific reason** for each deduction
- The **estimated or actual cost** of repair for each item
- A **check or money order** for the difference between the deposit and the damages claimed
- The following statement in **bold, 12-point font** (at least 4 points larger than surrounding text):

> "You must respond to this notice by mail within 7 days after receipt of same, otherwise you will forfeit the amount claimed for damages."

If this exact statement is missing or improperly formatted, the itemized list is defective under Michigan law.

---

## Tenant's 7-Day Response Window

After receiving an itemized damage list, the tenant has **7 days** to dispute the charges in writing.

- Disputes must be sent by mail
- If the tenant does not respond within 7 days, they forfeit the right to contest the charges

---

## Penalty for Landlord Non-Compliance

If a landlord fails to return the deposit or provide a proper itemized list within 30 days:
- The landlord **loses all right to withhold any portion** of the deposit
- The landlord may owe **twice (2×) the amount of the security deposit retained** (per MCL 554.613)
- Plus **attorney fees and court costs**

This is enforceable in small claims court — students do not necessarily need a lawyer to pursue this.

---

## What Landlords CAN Deduct

Legitimate deductions include:
- Physical damage beyond normal wear and tear (large carpet stains, holes in walls, broken fixtures, cracked tiles, broken windows, damaged countertops)
- Unpaid rent
- Unpaid utilities (only if the lease states utilities are the tenant's responsibility)

---

## What Landlords CANNOT Deduct

"Normal wear and tear" is not deductible. This includes:
- Minor scuff marks on walls
- Faded or slightly worn paint
- Worn carpeting from regular foot traffic
- Light scratches from normal furniture use

Landlords commonly charge students for these items. Knowing this distinction is your primary defense.

---

## Common Tactics Used Against Students

1. **Ignoring the forwarding address rule** — Landlords may claim the 30-day clock never started because they "didn't receive" a written address. Always send it certified mail.
2. **Charging for normal wear and tear** — Unreasonable cleaning fees, repainting charges, carpet replacement for minor wear.
3. **Missing the bold statement** — If the required bold disclaimer is absent from the damage notice, the entire itemized list is procedurally defective.
4. **Slow-walking the return** — Counting on students to not know the 30-day deadline, especially around graduation when they've already left town.

---

## Relevant Statutes

- **Act 348** — Michigan Security Deposit Act (the governing law)
- **MCL 554.602** — Security deposit limits (maximum 1.5× monthly rent)
- **MCL 554.609** — Itemized deduction procedures and required notice language
- **MCL 554.613** — Landlord's deadline to act and penalty for non-compliance (double damages + waiver)

---

## Student Action Checklist

- [ ] Before moving in: Document the unit with dated photos/video
- [ ] At move-out: Send forwarding address in writing via certified mail
- [ ] Day 31+: If no deposit or itemized list received, you have a legal claim
- [ ] If you receive a damage list: Respond in writing within 7 days if disputing
- [ ] File in small claims court for 2× damages if landlord is non-compliant
