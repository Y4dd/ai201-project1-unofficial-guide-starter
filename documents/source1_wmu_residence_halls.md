Source: wmich.edu/housing/options/halls and wmich.edu/housing/info/rates

## Residence Hall Overview

### Henry Hall
- Location: Central campus — 1–6 minute walk to Rec Center, Student Center, and dining
- Style: Traditional (community bathrooms)
- Eligibility: Any student
- Room types: Double and single rooms available
- Amenities: Renovated kitchen, updated game room, spacious lounges
- Price tier: $ (lower end)
- Meal plan: Required (see rates below)

### Valley Halls (Ackley/Shilling, Britton/Hadley)
- Location: Goldsworth Valley area — near Valley Dining Center, intramural fields, Goldsworth Valley Pond
- Style: Suite-style (two rooms connected by a shared bathroom)
- Eligibility: Any student; Britton/Hadley includes beginner/first-year options
- Special: Home to most of WMU's Living Learning Communities
- **Britton/Hadley exception: No meal plan required** — room-only billing
- Price tier: $
- Note: Garneau/Harvey and Eicher/LeFevre suites closed end of spring 2026

### Western Heights (Hall-Archer-Pickard East and West)
- Style: Innovative pod-based design — co-ed or single-gendered pods of 32–36 students
- Eligibility: First-year students only
- Each pod includes: kitchen, study area, social area, community + private bathroom options (staff-cleaned)
- Price tier: $$ (mid-range)
- Meal plan: Required

### Valley Oaks (Opening Fall 2026)
- Location: Heart of Goldsworth Valley
- Capacity: 1,000+ first-year students
- Style: New construction with biophilic design
- Room types: Single, double, and expanded single rooms
- Bathrooms: Community bathrooms shared by 3–6 residents (resident-cleaned)
- Features: Virtual learning spaces, air-conditioned with in-room PTAC units
- Eligibility: First-year students only
- Price tier: $$$ (highest)
- Meal plan: Required

## Additional Fees
- **Parking:** $300/year or $150/semester (full-time students); $130/semester (part-time)
- **Internet (ResNet):** Charged separately to student account

|------|-------|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|----------|
| Henry | Traditional | Community | No | Yes | Any student, central location |

| Western Heights | Pod | Community + private options (staff-cleaned) | Yes | Yes | First-year, community feel |

## Living Learning Communities (LLCs)
Located primarily in Valley Halls. These are themed residential communities where students with shared academic or interest groups live together. Eight specialized LLC programs are available.

---

## All-Gender Housing
Female, male, and all-gender options are available across residence hall selections.
