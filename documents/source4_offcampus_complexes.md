# Off-Campus Complex Deep Dives — WMU Student Housing

Sources: ApartmentRatings.com (108–164 reviews per complex), Yelp, complex websites, BBB (2024–2026)

## The Paddock

**Address:** 5900 Copper Beech Blvd, Kalamazoo, MI 49009
**Distance to WMU:** ~2 miles
**Distance to KVCC:** ~4 miles
**Unit sizes:** 650–2,000 sq ft
**Bussing:** Yes (KMetro bus stop on site)
**Website:** thepaddockkzoo.com

### Amenities
- Free Wi-Fi and cable included
- State-of-the-art fitness center with tanning beds
- Basketball and volleyball courts
- Hammock gardens at pond with grilling stations
- 24-hour community center (billiards, student lounge, business center)
- Amazon Hub package locker

### What Students Say

**Management — primarily negative:**
- "Management is possibly the worst in the business and refuse to acknowledge their own mistakes."
- Property manager accused of making promises and then making excuses for not following through.
- Payment processing issues resolved only when payments were sent via USPS overnight mail.
- One tenant waited four months for a mail key; management then tried to charge them for it.

**Move-in condition:**
- Units described as "extremely filthy" with broken furniture on move-in.
- "Cereal left under the bed and dog hair in the tub."
- Cleaning supplies scattered around the apartment; walls still being painted while residents were settling in.
- Took approximately one month to resolve initial move-in issues.

**Maintenance:**
- Bathrooms flood without warning.
- After flooding, maintenance makes residents wait for drying without fixing drywall damage.
- Water leaks to floors below with slow response.

**Electric bills:** The Paddock does NOT include electric/heat in rent. Unit sizes up to 2,000 sq ft combined with older construction means winter electric bills are a significant concern. Multiple student accounts cite high winter electric costs at this complex. (See Source 8 for Kalamazoo utility cost benchmarks.)

**Positive notes:** The amenity package is well-regarded. The pond and hammock area are popular. Some residents report quiet, pleasant experiences — reviews are polarized, not uniformly negative.

**Overall:** Historically popular with WMU students due to location and amenities, but management and maintenance complaints are consistent and numerous across multiple review platforms.

## 58 West (Luxury Student Living)

**Address:** 5800 Jefferson Commons Dr, Kalamazoo, MI 49009
**Distance to WMU:** ~2 miles
**Distance to KVCC:** ~4 miles
**KMetro bus:** Yes — on bus route
**Phone:** 269-544-1700
**Note on electric/heat:** Official site confirms fiber internet is included; electric and heat billing status is not stated — verify before signing.

### Amenities
- Fully furnished units
- High-speed fiber internet included
- Resort-style swimming pool and spa
- 24-hour fitness center
- Indoor all-sports court
- Hammock gardens
- Student lounge
- Floor plan range: studio to 4-bedroom units

### What Students Say

**Reviews are the most polarized of any Kalamazoo student complex:**

*Positive (strongly):** "One of the best living communities in Kalamazoo, especially for international students." Strong sense of community, events organized for residents, ability to meet people from around the world.

**Negative (strongly):** Described by some as "the WORST housing complex in Kalamazoo." Specific complaints:
- Major maintenance issues and pest infestations.
- Air conditioning failures — management told tenants to "open a window" when thermostat read 90°F.
- Repairs taking weeks with no follow-up.
- Staff members "appear nice but wait for residents to slip up to charge fees."

**What this split means:** 58 West's experience appears to depend heavily on which building or unit you are assigned, and on which management team was in place at the time. Check the most recent (post-2023) reviews specifically, as management has changed.

**Utilities:** Fiber internet included. Electric/heat billed separately.

## The Wyatt

**Address:** 5200 Croyden Ave, Kalamazoo, MI 49009
**Phone:** 269-344-7120
**Units:** 200 apartments and townhomes across 5 floor plans
**Bussing:** Free bussing to campus provided by complex

### Amenities
- Fully furnished rooms
- Private bathrooms for each bedroom (major differentiator)
- Free high-speed internet and cable included
- In-unit washer and dryer
- Resort-style swimming pool
- State-of-the-art fitness center

### What Students Say

**Noise — consistent complaint:**
- Walls are "incredibly thin" and apartments are poorly insulated for sound.
- If you have loud neighbors, you will hear them.
- Not well-insulated against outside noise either.

**Security — criticized:**
- Security cars drive around giving "the appearance that it is a safe community" but guards "don't really DO anything."
- Multiple weekend incidents with glass broken in parking lots from parties.
- Management response to security threats described as slow or inadequate.

**Management change — recent positive note:**
- "The new management is outstanding and I am super pleased thus far living at The Wyatt." Multiple recent residents note improvement under new management (post-2023/2024).
- Longer-term residents (pre-management change) had more mixed experiences.

**Location context:** The Wyatt is positioned just behind West Main Street, somewhat shielded from street noise. It is on or near the KMetro bus route. The location is car-dependent for most errands.

**Utilities:** Internet and cable included. Electric/heat billed separately.

**Best fit:** Students who want furnished, private bathrooms, and are social/unbothered by noise. Not recommended for students who need quiet to study or who are light sleepers.

## The Bronco Club

**Address:** 3201 Michigamme Woods Dr, Kalamazoo, MI 49006
**ApartmentRatings:** 108 reviews, 3.5/5 stars overall — but 1/5 stars for staff, grounds, and noise
**BBB:** Has business profile
**Unit range:** 2–6 bedroom townhomes
**Status:** Newly renovated

### Amenities
- Community pool and volleyball court
- 24-hour gym
- Off-leash dog park
- Community clubhouse
- Close walking distance to WMU

### What Students Say

**Location:** Walking distance to WMU campus — a significant advantage. Adjacent to Fraternity Village Drive and the Greek housing area.

**Noise — severe:**
- Consistently rated 1/5 for noise. Expected, per residents, "because it is right by fraternity village."
- If you want quiet, this is explicitly the wrong complex.

**Staff and maintenance — poor:**
- Staff rated 1/5 by ApartmentRatings aggregation.
- Grounds rated 1/5 for upkeep and appearance.
- One recent resident (2024–2025) called it "the worst rental experience I've ever had. From day one, living conditions were unacceptable and management was unresponsive."

**Lease:** Described as "rather strict and tedious."

**Positive notes:** Some residents love it for the community atmosphere, walkability to campus, and affordable rates. The pool/volleyball/gym package and dog park are genuine amenities. Social students who want to be in the thick of campus life rate it highly.

**Safety note:** A fatal shooting occurred in January 2015 at **Copper Beech Townhomes** (2804 W Michigan Ave) — a separate complex from The Bronco Club. The Bronco Club's previous name was West Michigan Woods. Check current crime data at Kalamazoo Public Safety for the Michigamme Woods area.

**Best fit:** Social students, Greek life-adjacent residents, students who prioritize walkability to campus over peace and quiet.

## Hunter's Ridge

**Address:** 4130 W Michigan Ave, Kalamazoo, MI 49006
**Distance to WMU:** ~2.5 miles
**Units:** 294 apartments, 3 stories (built 1997)
**Unit sizes:** 500–750 sq ft
**Floor plans:** 1-bedroom and 2-bedroom
**Rent:** Starting at ~$555–$610 per bed
**Bussing:** KMetro Route 3 (West Michigan) runs along W Michigan Ave — check stop proximity before signing
**Website:** huntersridgewmu.com

### Amenities
- Private bedrooms and bathrooms per unit
- In-unit washer and dryer
- Balcony or patio
- Covered parking
- Fully equipped kitchen
- Fitness center
- Pet-friendly (restricted breeds: Pit Bulls, Rottweilers, German Shepherds, and others — verify current list)

### What Students Say

**ApartmentRatings: 2.5/5 overall (70 reviews)**

**Management — highly polarized:**
- Some residents report friendly, helpful office staff.
- Significant complaints: management described as "extremely rude," staff "NEVER call you back," and high employee turnover means you may deal with different people each time.
- One resident noted difficulty obtaining lease copies and apartment information.

**Maintenance — slow:**
- Rated approximately 3/5 — below average.
- Specific complaint: maintenance requests going unanswered for 3+ months (e.g., storage unit lock changes pending indefinitely).

**Noise — consistent complaint:**
- Rated 2/5 for noise.
- "Hallways smell like weed 24/7."
- Near-constant noise attributed to a high density of pet owners — "almost every apartment has a dog."

**Positive notes:** Private bathrooms, in-unit laundry, and covered parking are genuine differentiators at this price point. Some residents report quiet, positive experiences — reviews are polarized.

**Utilities included:** High-speed internet, cable, water, trash removal, and sewer are included in rent. Electric and heat are billed separately — verify before signing.

**Best fit:** Students who want private bathrooms and in-unit laundry at a lower price point than newer complexes, and who can tolerate inconsistent management and noise.

## The Tate on Howard

**Address:** 700 S Howard St, Kalamazoo, MI 49006
**Distance to WMU:** Walking distance — directly across Howard Street from WMU's Haworth College of Business
**Unit sizes:** 1,374–1,578 sq ft
**Floor plans:** 3-bedroom and 4-bedroom (individually-keyed bedrooms)
**Rent:** Starting at ~$484–$660 per bed depending on floor plan
**Bussing:** KMetro Route 21 (Solon/Kendall/Lafayette) runs along Howard St — direct to campus loading zone
**Phone:** (269) 743-3531
**Website:** thetateonhoward.com

### Amenities
- Fiber-based internet included
- In-unit washer and dryer in every home
- Individually-keyed bedrooms
- Resort-style pool with sun deck and hot tub
- 24-hour fitness center
- Study areas and quiet resident lounge
- Coffee bar
- Hammock garden and dog park
- Covered, secured parking garage (free and reserved options)
- Spacious patios and balconies

### What Students Say

**ApartmentRatings: 133 reviews**

**Location — major advantage:**
- Described as "steps from the WMU campus" and directly across Howard Street from the business college.
- KMetro Route 21 stop on Howard provides a direct no-transfer ride to the main campus loading zone.

**Management — generally positive but parking complaints:**
- Staff described as "very kind and always available" in multiple reviews.
- One significant complaint: a vehicle was towed without notice; resident was charged $300 to retrieve it despite having a temporary parking pass. Management blamed the resident.

**Maintenance — inconsistent:**
- A/C unit failures in summer with long wait times for repairs reported by multiple residents.
- Maintenance staff generally on-site but response time for non-emergency issues varies.

**Noise:**
- Howard Street corridor has higher ambient noise than complexes further from campus. Expect foot traffic and street noise, particularly on weekends.

**Positive notes:** Premium amenities (fiber internet, pool with hot tub, secured garage, study lounge) at a competitive per-bed price. Location is the strongest selling point for students who want to minimize commute.

**Utilities:** Fiber internet confirmed as included. Electric, heat, and water billing status — verify before signing.

**Best fit:** Students who prioritize walkability to campus and premium amenities. Strong choice for business college students specifically, given the across-the-street location to Haworth.
