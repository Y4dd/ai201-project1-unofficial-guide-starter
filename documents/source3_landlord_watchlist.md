## Edward Rose & Sons — Concord Place Apartments

**Address:** 1400 Concord Place Dr, Kalamazoo, MI 49009
**Distance to WMU:** ~3.7 miles / 8 minutes by car
**Managed by:** Edward Rose & Sons (large regional property management company)
**Unit range:** Studio, 1–2 bedroom | Rent: ~$766–$1,305/month
**ApartmentRatings profile:** 155 reviews

### What Students Say

**Price:** Widely acknowledged as one of the cheapest options in Kalamazoo. Multiple reviews confirm it "reflects that." Good first apartment if budget is the top priority.

**Maintenance — split reviews:**
- Negative: A mold ticket was left open for several months with no response. Pipes in studio apartments "crack constantly at loud volumes in the winter and will wake you up" despite multiple maintenance requests.
- Positive: Some residents found maintenance "always done such a great job whenever I need something fixed."

**Pest issues:** Multiple residents reported roaches, with one noting they saw them "pretty much every day."

**Noise and safety:** The complex has been described as having a high crime rate and being unsafe in parking lots at night. Significant noise at all hours reported by multiple tenants.

**Grounds:** Dumpsters overflow frequently; excessive dog waste in grass areas.

**Bottom line from tenants:** "Probably the cheapest place in Kalamazoo which is great but it definitely reflects that." For students on a tight budget who are aware of the tradeoffs, it is functional. For students prioritizing safety or quiet study environments, it is frequently cautioned against.

## Icon Properties

**Type:** Locally owned and operated property management company
**Operating since:** 1990 (35+ years in the Kalamazoo area per BBB)
**Properties:** Single-family homes, duplexes, apartments, student housing — spread across Kalamazoo, Portage, and surrounding areas
**Student housing concentration:** Near Fraternity Village Drive, close to WMU
**BBB:** Accredited (since December 5, 2015)
**Birdeye rating:** 3.1/5 stars (110 Birdeye reviews; 129 total across platforms)

### What Students Say

**Student housing specifics:** Fraternity Village Drive townhomes feature 4 bedrooms, 3 bathrooms, large living rooms, updated kitchens, and full-size washer/dryer.

**Pros:** Locally owned means decisions can sometimes be made faster than with large corporate landlords. Proximity to WMU and Fraternity Village is a selling point.

**Cons:** Mixed customer service reviews. Some tenants report difficulty reaching management; others describe smooth interactions.

**Yelp listing note:** One Kalamazoo Icon Properties location is listed as "CLOSED" on Yelp — verify the specific property address before signing.

### Watch for
- Icon manages many different property types — quality varies significantly by individual property. Ask specifically about the unit's maintenance history before signing.

## Nu Partners

**Type:** Property management company, Kalamazoo area
**Publicly available reviews:** Limited indexed online data — no significant public review trail found on major platforms.

### Why This Matters
The absence of easily findable reviews is itself informative. Students have raised concerns in local housing discussions about this company. If you are considering a Nu Partners property:
- Request references from current tenants
- Ask specifically about security deposit return practices (see Source 7 for Michigan law)
- Ask about lease break terms and fees in writing before signing
- Google the exact property address (not just the company name) for unit-specific reviews

## General Kalamazoo Landlord Red Flags (applies to all)

Based on aggregated student reports:

1. **Deposit disputes at move-out** — The most common complaint across all Kalamazoo student housing. Always document the unit with dated photos and video before moving in and on the day you move out.

2. **Slow-walking maintenance** — Many complexes are responsive to initial requests but go silent after. Keep a written log (emails/texts) of every maintenance request.

3. **Management turnover** — Several complexes (The Wyatt, 58 West) have changed management companies. Reviews from 2–3 years ago may not reflect current conditions. Check for recent reviews specifically.

4. **Move-in condition** — Multiple Kalamazoo student properties have documented reports of dirty or unfinished units on move-in day. Do a walkthrough before accepting keys and document everything in writing.

5. **Corporate vs. local** — Large corporate managers (Edward Rose) have standardized processes but impersonal service. Local managers (Icon) may be more flexible but less consistent across properties.
